Rugby 101 – From Scrum to Try

Rugby is a full-contact, high-intensity sport where two teams battle to carry, pass, and kick an oval ball over the try line. It’s a mix of strategy, bravery, and controlled chaos.

What Is Rugby?

Rugby started in England in the 19th century and has grown into a global sport played by millions. Two teams of 15 players compete on a rectangular grass field with H-shaped goalposts at each end. The main goal is to score more points than the opponent before the match ends.

Unlike American football, there are no forward passes. The ball can only be passed sideways or backward, so teams have to run in support and work together to move the ball up the field.

How Do You Play?

Scoring Points

  • Try (5 pts) – Touch the ball down in the opponent’s in-goal area.
  • Conversion (2 pts) – Kick the ball through the posts after a try.
  • Penalty Goal (3 pts) – Kick at goal after the other team commits a penalty.
  • Drop Goal (3 pts) – Drop-kick the ball through the posts during open play.

Key Rules in Simple Terms

  • No forward passes – only sideways or backward.
  • You must tackle below the shoulders and wrap with your arms (no spear tackles).
  • When you’re tackled, you have to release the ball so play can continue.
  • Teams contest possession with rucks, mauls, lineouts, and scrums.

Positions: Forwards vs Backs

Each of the 15 players has a specific role. Forwards are usually bigger and focus on winning the ball. Backs are typically faster and focus on creating and finishing scoring opportunities.

Group Position Main Job
Forwards Power Props & Hooker Anchor the scrum, protect the ball, and provide physical strength.
Forwards Locks Win lineout balls and add drive to the scrum.
Forwards Flankers & Number 8 Hunt the ball, tackle everything, and link with the backs.
Backs Speed Scrum-half & Fly-half Control the attack, make decisions, and kick for territory.
Backs Centers Break the defensive line with strong running and defense.
Backs Wings & Fullback Finish scoring chances and field kicks from the opposition.

Fun Facts & Why Rugby Is Awesome

  1. Rugby is built on respect – players always call the referee “sir” or “ma’am.”
  2. Every position needs a different body type, so there’s literally a role for everyone.
  3. Even after brutal tackles, players help each other up. Hard hits, but big respect.

Whether you’re into big hits, clever strategy, or crazy athleticism, rugby delivers. Once you understand the basic rules, it’s one of the most addictive sports to watch (and play).
